# Elon Musk v. OpenAI, Inc. et al. (Case No. 3:24-cv-00781)

## Case Overview
- **Plaintiff**: Elon Musk (Co-founder, early investor)
- **Defendants**: OpenAI, Inc., OpenAI LP, Sam Altman, Greg Brockman
- **Court**: U.S. District Court, Northern District of California
- **Filed**: February 29, 2024
- **Status**: Motion to dismiss DENIED (January 2026) - Proceeding to trial

## Factual Background

### Founding and Original Mission (2015-2018)
- **December 2015**: OpenAI founded as 501(c)(3) nonprofit
- **Founding Agreement**: AI developed for the benefit of humanity
- **Key Commitment**: Technology to remain open-source and freely available
- **Musk Contribution**: Approximately $44 million in donations
- **Board Members**: Musk, Altman, Brockman, Sutskever, others

### The Pivot (2019-2023)
- **March 2019**: Created OpenAI LP (capped-profit subsidiary)
- **July 2019**: Microsoft invests $1 billion
- **January 2023**: Microsoft invests additional $10 billion
- **November 2023**: Altman briefly ousted, then reinstated
- **2024-2025**: Discussions to convert fully to for-profit

### Musk Departure
- **February 2018**: Musk resigns from board
- **Stated Reason**: Potential conflict with Tesla AI efforts
- **Alleged Reason**: Disagreement over commercialization direction

## Legal Claims

### Count I: Breach of Contract
- Founding Agreement constituted binding contract
- OpenAI agreed to develop AGI for public benefit
- Exclusive Microsoft license violates open-source commitment
- GPT-4 and subsequent models kept proprietary

### Count II: Breach of Fiduciary Duty
- Altman and Brockman owed fiduciary duties to nonprofit mission
- Self-dealing through for-profit restructuring
- Personal enrichment at expense of charitable purpose

### Count III: Unfair Business Practices (Cal. Bus. and Prof. Code 17200)
- Solicited donations under false pretenses
- Bait-and-switch from nonprofit to for-profit
- Competitive harm to other AI developers

### Count IV: Accounting
- Demand for full accounting of use of nonprofit donations
- Valuation of IP transferred to for-profit
- Microsoft deal terms and equity distributions

## Defendants Arguments

### Contract Defense
- No enforceable contract existed
- Founding documents were aspirational, not binding
- Musk waived claims by resigning from board

### Fiduciary Duty Defense
- Musk was not a beneficiary of nonprofit
- Business judgment rule protects board decisions
- Restructuring was necessary for survival

### Standing Defense
- Musk lacks standing as mere donor
- No cognizable injury
- Claims belong to Attorney General, not private plaintiff

## Court Ruling on Motion to Dismiss (January 2026)

**DENIED** - Court found:
1. Sufficient factual allegations of contract formation
2. Genuine dispute over nature of founding commitments
3. Discovery needed to determine what Musk was promised
4. Whether board breached duties to nonprofit mission
5. Extent of Microsoft control over OpenAI technology

## Key Legal Questions for Trial

1. **Contract Formation**: Did the founding documents create enforceable obligations?
2. **Consideration**: Was Musk donation sufficient consideration for promises made?
3. **Breach**: Did the Microsoft deal and for-profit pivot breach those obligations?
4. **Damages**: What are Musk actual damages, if any?
5. **Public Interest**: Should AI development promises be legally enforceable?

## Relevant Precedents

### Supporting Plaintiff
- **Smithers v. St. Luke Roosevelt Hospital** - Donor standing to enforce gift restrictions
- **Carl J. Herzog Foundation v. University of Bridgeport** - Cy pres doctrine limits
- **Stern v. Lucy Webb Hayes** - Nonprofit director fiduciary duties

### Supporting Defendants
- **Madden v. Kaiser Foundation Hospitals** - Business judgment rule for nonprofits
- **Queen of Angels Hospital v. Younger** - AG exclusive enforcement of charitable trusts
- **Holt v. College of Osteopathic Physicians** - Donor intent vs organizational discretion

## Trial Implications

### If Musk Wins
- Potential unwinding of Microsoft deal
- Forced return to nonprofit structure
- Precedent for donor enforcement of mission statements
- Chilling effect on nonprofit to for-profit conversions

### If OpenAI Wins
- Validates capped-profit model
- Confirms aspirational nature of founding documents
- Limits donor standing in nonprofit governance
- Green light for similar AI lab restructurings

## Jury Considerations

### Factors Favoring Musk
- Clear public statements about open-source mission
- Significant personal investment ($44M)
- Timing of pivot after Musk departure
- Public perception of bait-and-switch

### Factors Favoring OpenAI
- Musk voluntary resignation
- No written contract with specific terms
- Nonprofit survival required capital
- Musk competing AI ventures (xAI) suggest ulterior motive
